Present Levels of Academic Achievement and Functional Performance (PLAAFP)
Jordan is a friendly, motivated student who participates actively in class discussions. Classroom assessments and teacher observations show strengths in verbal reasoning, science curiosity, and peer collaboration. Jordan reads grade-level passages with reduced accuracy and fluency, especially with multisyllabic words and unfamiliar vocabulary. Current oral reading fluency is 74 correct words per minute on 4th grade text (benchmark: 115 cwpm). Reading comprehension is stronger when text is read aloud and when graphic organizers are provided. Jordan benefits from frequent movement breaks, chunked directions, and visual schedules.

Parent Concerns
Parents report that homework completion takes significantly longer than expected and that Jordan becomes frustrated with independent reading. Parents request improved home-school communication and explicit instruction for reading strategies.

Measurable Annual Goals
Goal 1 - Reading Fluency:
By April 2027, given 4th-5th grade passages, Jordan will increase oral reading fluency from 74 to 105 correct words per minute with 95% accuracy across 3 consecutive probes.
Progress Monitoring: Curriculum-based measurement every 2 weeks.

Goal 2 - Decoding and Word Study:
By April 2027, when presented with multisyllabic words, Jordan will apply taught syllable division and morphology strategies to decode words with 85% accuracy across 4 consecutive sessions.
Progress Monitoring: Weekly teacher-created probes.

Goal 3 - Self-Management:
By April 2027, during independent work periods, Jordan will use a self-monitoring checklist and return to task within 1 minute after a prompt in 80% of observed opportunities across 4 weeks.
Progress Monitoring: Behavior data collected twice weekly.

Special Education and Related Services
1) Specialized Academic Instruction (Reading): 45 minutes/session, 4 sessions/week, small group, push-in/pull-out hybrid.
2) Executive Function Support: 30 minutes/session, 2 sessions/week, small group.
3) School Counseling (self-advocacy and coping skills): 20 minutes/session, 1 session/week.

Supplementary Aids, Services, and Program Modifications
- Preferential seating near instruction area.
- Extended time (1.5x) for classroom assessments.
- Directions read aloud and provided in written form.
- Assignments chunked into smaller tasks with check-ins.
- Access to text-to-speech for grade-level reading tasks.
- Graphic organizers for reading responses and writing tasks.
- Movement break every 25-30 minutes as needed.

Participation with Non-Disabled Peers
Jordan will participate in the general education classroom for at least 85% of the school day.

State and District Testing Accommodations
- Small group setting.
- Extended time (1.5x).
- Text-to-speech for directions and reading passages where permitted.
- Frequent supervised breaks.

Progress Reporting
Progress toward annual goals will be reported to parents every 9 weeks with data summaries and instructional adjustments.
